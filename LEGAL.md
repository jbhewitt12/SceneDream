## Copyright and Fair Use

SceneDream is a tool for personal, non-commercial use. It processes text from source documents you supply and uses that text to generate original AI images. No original text is reproduced or redistributed by the tool.

Under United States copyright law, use of copyrighted material may qualify as **fair use** (17 U.S.C. § 107) when evaluated against four factors:

1. **Purpose and character of the use** — Personal, non-commercial use that produces a transformative output (visual art derived from prose) weighs in favour of fair use.
2. **Nature of the copyrighted work** — Published creative works are generally afforded strong protection, which weighs against fair use.
3. **Amount of the work used** — SceneDream extracts short scene descriptions from larger works rather than reproducing whole chapters or the complete text.
4. **Effect on the market** — AI-generated images derived from scene descriptions are unlikely to substitute for or harm the market for the original book.

No court has ruled definitively on AI image generation from literary works. The fair use analysis above represents the strongest available argument under current US law, but it is not a guarantee.

## What this means in practice

- **Personal use**: Generating images for your own enjoyment is the lowest-risk use case.
- **Sharing outputs**: Sharing individual images non-commercially (e.g. social media, personal portfolio) is generally considered low risk, though you should credit the source work.
- **Commercial use**: Selling, licensing, or otherwise commercially exploiting images generated from copyrighted works is higher risk and not something this tool is designed for.
