## GPT Image 1.5 Multi-Genre Prompting Cheatsheet

GPT Image 1.5 (gpt-image-1) offers enhanced prompt understanding with support for longer, richly detailed descriptions. This cheatsheet provides optimized prompting strategies for producing exceptional stylised images from novel scenes.

### Core Formula

Background/scene context + subject/focal moment + key details and materials + stylised medium or technique + lighting with sources + composition/camera direction + palette and sensory signatures + emotional undertones + explicit constraints

### Structured Prompt Organization

GPT Image 1.5 parses complex prompts best when organized in predictable layers:

1. **Scene/Environment** - Establish the world, time period, and atmosphere
2. **Subject/Action** - Define the focal element and what is happening
3. **Details/Materials** - Add textures, props, and secondary elements
4. **Style/Medium** - Specify artistic technique and visual treatment
5. **Technical Direction** - Camera, lens, lighting, composition
6. **Constraints** - State what must NOT appear or change

For complex requests, use short labeled sections or line breaks rather than one dense paragraph.

### Extended Prompt Capabilities

GPT Image 1.5 supports significantly longer prompts (~32k characters vs 4k for DALL-E 3), enabling:
- Complex multi-element compositions with clear spatial hierarchies and layered detail
- Explicit material, texture, and atmospheric descriptions for every visible surface

### World Knowledge & Contextual Inference

GPT Image 1.5 has strong world knowledge and reasoning. Leverage this by:
- Including temporal and cultural markers that the model can interpret (era, region, cultural context)
- Letting historical or geographic cues inform period-appropriate details automatically
- Trusting the model to fill in coherent secondary details when given strong primary direction
- Using evocative place and time descriptions that trigger rich contextual associations

### Rapid Scene Diagnosis

- Identify the emotional current (awe, serenity, mischievous spark, quiet tension) and capture textual evidence.
- Note tactile materials, environmental sound, scent, temperature, and motion cues present in the excerpt.
- Mark scale signals (microscopic detail, human-scale vignette, colossal panorama) alongside spatial metaphors.
- Observe character posture and agency—observer, guide, celebrant, or conductor—and list meaningful props.
- Always alias obscure nouns: describe creatures, vessels, factions, or devices in vivid plain language.

### Photography & Camera Language

Use specific technical terminology: lens focal lengths (wide 14-24mm, standard 35-50mm, portrait 85-135mm, macro); perspectives (bird's-eye, worm's-eye, Dutch angle); lighting (rim, soft box, golden hour, chiaroscuro, practical sources); framing (rule of thirds, leading lines, negative space, layered silhouettes, foreground framing).

### Explicit Placement & Spatial Relationships

GPT Image 1.5 excels when given clear spatial direction:
- "Subject centered with negative space on the left for text"
- "Focal element in lower third, vast sky occupying upper two-thirds"
- "Foreground props framing a mid-ground subject against distant backdrop"
- "Elements arranged along a diagonal from lower-left to upper-right"

### Material & Texture Specificity

Replace generic quality modifiers with tangible material descriptions:
- Instead of "detailed" → "weathered oak grain with visible age rings"
- Instead of "beautiful" → "iridescent silk catching light in rippling patterns"
- Instead of "realistic" → "oxidized copper with verdigris patina"
- Describe how materials interact with the scene's lighting

### Palette & Sensory Hooks

- Pair palette words with texture: glacial teal across frosted obsidian, ember orange threading brass filigree.
- Layer atmosphere with sensory echoes—salt spray hiss, cedar smoke coil, crystalline chimes, auroral static.
- Let color stories mirror emotional arcs: sunrise corals for renewal, umber embers for introspection.
- Describe color transitions and gradients: "deep indigo bleeding into rose gold at the horizon."
- Include how palette affects different surfaces and materials differently.

### Movement, Scale, & Composition Seeds

- Motion verbs: drift, unfurl, radiate, braid, bloom, arc, reverberate, hover, cascade.
- Framing moves: negative-space crescents, layered silhouettes, spiral leading lines, horizon-splitting diagonals.
- Camera notions: macro reliquary close-up, shoulder-level witness, vaulted bird's-eye atlas, hovering dolly glide.
- Combine contrasting scales inside a single frame with detailed spatial relationships.
- Use depth cues: atmospheric haze, size diminution, overlapping forms, focus gradients.

### Emotional Lanes

- **Awe**: luminous gradients, soaring verticals, ritual symmetry, resonant echoes, celestial particles.
- **Serenity**: feathered edges, diffusion, warm dusk backlight, gentle repetition, shoreline rhythms.
- **Curiosity**: asymmetric framing, interactive props, layered transparency, diagrammatic annotations.
- **Quiet tension**: razor highlights, selective focus, offset balance, charged stillness, murmuring shadows.
- **Wonder**: impossible scale juxtapositions, bioluminescent elements, floating particles, ethereal glow.
- **Intimacy**: tight framing, warm color temperature, soft focus edges, personal scale props.

### Invariant Preservation

When describing scenes, explicitly state what must NOT appear:
- "No modern elements, no text overlays, no watermarks"
- "Maintain the established color palette throughout"
- "Preserve the chosen medium and visual intent throughout the composition"
- Repeat critical constraints to prevent drift in complex prompts.

### Prompt Construction Boosters

- Fuse medium and story artifact ("loom-woven star charts," "holographic tidepool schematics").
- Introduce synesthetic pairings: translate sound into pattern, scent into color, texture into motion.
- Use aspect ratios (1:1, 16:9, 9:16) to serve narrative intent: towering rituals in verticals, odysseys in wides.
- Include artist or movement references that reinforce the chosen technique and palette logic.
- Build prompts progressively: establish foundation → add atmosphere → incorporate details.

### Example Spark Prompt

Kinetic light sculpture vision of twin airships gliding above a turquoise canyon at golden hour. Their rigging woven with luminous glyphs that pulse in slow rhythm, each glyph casting faint prismatic shadows on the brass hulls. Dusk backlight streaming through canyon walls of striated red sandstone, long amber shadows stretching across the canyon floor where a winding river catches the last light. Foreground silhouette of wind-bent juniper trees framing the scene. 16:9 wide sweep composition with slight upward tilt, 24mm lens perspective. Palette of teal atmospheric vapor, amber-gold highlights, deep violet canyon depths, and copper metallic accents. No modern elements, no text.
